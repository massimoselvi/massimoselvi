### **The STAR Response**

**Situation:**
At **Toky Digital** (or your most relevant previous role), we managed a legacy monolith supporting high-volume digital marketing campaigns. As traffic grew, the system suffered from scaling bottlenecks and high operational costs. We needed to migrate to a modern, scalable architecture while the business continued to launch new features.

**Task:**
My task was to architect and lead the migration to an **AWS Serverless Event-Driven Architecture (EDA)**. I needed to ensure data isolation between users, improve deployment speed, and maintain a high standard of code quality through **TDD** and **Domain-Driven Design (DDD)**.

**Action:**

* **Architectural Strategy:** I used DDD to identify bounded contexts and broke the monolith into microservices. I chose **AWS EventBridge** as the backbone to decouple services and used **AWS AppSync (GraphQL)** to provide a unified entry point for the frontend.
* **Infrastructure & Automation:** I defined the entire stack using **AWS CDK**. To manage the growing number of Lambda functions, I implemented a **monorepo using TurboRepo and pnpm workspaces**. This allowed us to share TypeScript interfaces and utility libraries across the team seamlessly, significantly improving **Developer Experience (DX)**.
* **Security & Data Isolation:** Following the principle of least privilege, I implemented per-tenant data isolation within **DynamoDB using Single-Table Design**. I also integrated **Cognito and OIDC** to ensure secure, authenticated access.
* **CI/CD & Quality:** I established resilient pipelines using **GitHub Actions**. I insisted on a **TDD approach**, ensuring that every Lambda function was supported by unit and integration tests before deployment. This shifted our quality "left" and reduced production incidents.
* **Servant Leadership:** As the interim CTO, I didn't just dictate the architecture; I empowered the team through **pair programming and mob programming sessions**, ensuring everyone understood the new serverless paradigms and the monorepo workflow.

**Result:**
The migration resulted in a **40% reduction in operational costs** and a significant increase in scalability—we could now handle traffic spikes that previously caused downtime. Our deployment frequency increased from once a week to multiple times per day thanks to the **TurboRepo/GitHub Actions** pipeline. Most importantly, the team’s morale improved as the "monolith fear" was replaced by a modern, automated, and observable environment.
